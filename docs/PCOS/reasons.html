My PCOS journey

I did not want admit there was something wrong but I knew there was something wrong

After not getting my period consistently for 3 months I knew I should get it checked out. My mom just finished getting emergency brain surgery for an brain aneurysm and was scared that the gynecologist will find something.

I was already being my mom emotional support, 3 jobs and school, emotionally I didn't want anything else to be added to my load.

\ Image placeholder 1

I put my big girl pants on and went to gynocologist on the far end of the East Side in the pouring rain. I'll spare you the details but one pee test and blood test later I was diagnosed with PCOS

Image placeholder 2

I didn't like that gynocologist though, so one turned into two and I went to my mom's gynocologist WHO IS A MAN which I thought was weird but whatever. He reccommended me to take metformin for 6 months which was making me nauseous so I stopped taking it and went to a third gyno.

Image placeholder 3

This third gyno was perfect! She recommended a natural supplement Inositol for me to take that didn't make me nauseous and was willing to help me to explore more natural methods!

Image placeholder 4

So what's next? I am still figuring out if Inositol is the best solution to me and talking to other people who have PCOS to see if something else would be better!

All from the mind and heart of Kayley Chery
